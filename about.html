About Ozamiz

Ozamiz City, located in the province of Misamis Occidental in northern Mindanao, Philippines, is a thriving city known as the "Gem of Panguil Bay." With its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and warm community spirit, the city serves as a center for trade, education, and culture in the region. Surrounded by lush mountains, scenic coastal areas, and the tranquil waters of Panguil Bay, Ozamiz offers both residents and visitors a harmonious blend of urban convenience and rural charm. It is a city where progress meets tradition, providing a welcoming atmosphere and a strong sense of community.

History background

The city of Ozamiz grew out of an old Spanish town called Misamis—a name believed to have been derived from the Subanen word kuyamis, a variety of coconut. Other unverified historical sources, however, suggest that the name Misamis came from the Spanish word Misa (Catholic Mass). The old Spanish town grew in size due to the nearby Spanish garrison stationed at the stone fort named Fuerte de la Concepcion y del Triunfo. The fort was constructed some time in 1756 in order to attempt to combat the pirate activities originating in the nearby Lanao area. In 1850, the town of Misamis became the capital of the District of Misamis.

Ozamiz Famous For

Ozamiz City is famous for its Historic Fort Santiago (Cotta Fort), a Spanish-era fort built in 1755 that symbolizes the city’s rich colonial history and attracts many tourists. The city is also well-known for the Birhen sa Cotta Shrine, a religious site celebrated for its miraculous stories and visited by pilgrims from across the country. Nature enthusiasts are drawn to the Malindang Mountain Range, a haven for adventurers offering trekking, breathtaking views, and rich biodiversity. Ozamiz comes alive during the Subayan Keg Subanon Festival, a vibrant celebration showcasing the colorful traditions, dances, and music of the indigenous Subanon tribe. Panguil Bay adds to the city’s allure with its scenic beauty and vital role as a hub for trade and transportation. Food lovers enjoy the city’s delicious local cuisine, especially its fresh seafood and native delicacies that reflect the flavors of Mindanao. Finally, Ozamiz is known for its warm and welcoming locals, whose hospitality leaves every visitor feeling at home.
